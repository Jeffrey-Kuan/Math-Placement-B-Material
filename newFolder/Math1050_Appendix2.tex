Determine whether the statement is always true, sometimes true, or never true.

\begin{enumerate}
  \item An even integer is a multiple of 2 . Always true
  \item When $10 d$ is used to represent the value of $d$ dimes, 10 is written as the coefficient of $d$ because it is the number of cents in one dime. Always true
  \item Given the consecutive odd integers -5 and -3 , the next consecutive odd integer is -1 . Always true
  \item If the first of three consecutive odd integers is $n$, then the second and third consecutive odd integers are represented as $n+1$ and $n+3$. Never true
  \item You have a total of 20 coins in nickels and dimes. If $n$ represents the number of nickels you have, then $n-20$ represents the number of dimes you have. Never true
  \item You have a total of seven coins in dimes and quarters. The total value of the coins is $\$ 1.15$. If $d$ represents the number of dimes you have, then $10 d+25(7-d)=1.15$. Never true
\end{enumerate}

Recall that the perimeter of a plane geometric figure is a measure of the distance around the figure. Perimeter is used, for example, in buying fencing for a lawn and in determining how much baseboard is needed for a room.\\
The perimeter of a triangle is the sum of the lengths of the three sides. Therefore, if $a, b$, and c represent the lengths of the sides of a triangle, the perimeter, $P$, of the triangle is given by $\boldsymbol{P}=\boldsymbol{a}+\boldsymbol{b}+\boldsymbol{c}$.

\section*{Instructor Note}
The problems in this section are only some of the geometry problems that can be found throughout the text.

The word perimeter is made up of two parts, periand meter. Meter indicates measure. For example, a speedometer is used to measure speed. Peri- means "around." A periscope is used to look around. Thus perimeter is the measure around a figure.

The word isosceles is based on the Greek word isos, which means "same." In an isosceles triangle, two sides are of the same length.

The word equilateral consists of two parts: equi-, meaning "equal," and lateral, meaning "side." Students may be familiar with the word lateral in other contexts, such as a lateral line on the side of a fish or a lateral pass in football.
